Florian Clemente

artwork

I grew up in Munich where I studied Bioinformatics jointly at the Technical- and Ludwig-Maximilian University. My interests in natural sciences and evolution led me to do a Ph.D. in Population Genetics in Vienna. It was during that time that I also became more interested in arts, likely being influenced by the rich cultural program in Vienna. As a result, I started pencil drawing, mainly working on portraits and figures. After my graduation, my scientific career further brought me to Cambridge, UK as postdoctoral researcher and my artwork came to a halt. It was only during the time of my next postdoctoral position in Montpellier in the South of France that I rediscovered my passion for arts. There I got to see landscapes and places around Arles, Saint-RÃ©my-de-Provence, and Saintes-Maries-de-la-Mer, of which Vincent Van Gogh painted some of his most famous works. Seeing these original locations inspired me so much that, for the first time since school, I took a brush into my hands. Initially with acrylic paint and later with oil colors, I tried to teach myself to handle a brush, aiming to pick up at least a bit of Vincent's so touching style. Over the last three years, I have gained some experience and even though I might never be able to paint like Vincent, I hope that my artistic work will further improve and might find its own way and justification.
