Florian Clemente

artwork

I grew up in Munich where I studied Bioinformatics jointly at the Technical University and Ludwig-Maximilian University. My interests in natural sciences and evolution led me to do a Ph.D. in Population Genetics in Vienna. It was during this time that I also became increasingly interested in art, likely due to the influence of the rich cultural program in Vienna. As a result I revived my pencil drawing, mainly focusing on acquiring skills for accomplishing portraits and figures. After my graduation, my scientific career brought me to Cambridge in the UK as a postdoctoral researcher and my artwork came to a halt. It was only during my next postdoctoral position in Montpellier in the South of France that I rediscovered my passion for art. There I got to see landscapes and places around Arles, Saint-RÃ©my-de-Provence, and Saintes-Maries-de-la-Mer, of which Vincent Van Gogh painted some of his most famous works. Getting to know the actual locations where these paintings were made inspired me so much that for the first time since high school, I decided to take a brush into my hands. Initially with acrylic paint and later with oil colors, I tried to teach myself, aiming to pick up at least a bit of Vincent's so touching style. During the last three years I have gained some experience in this, and even though I might never be able to paint like Vincent himself, the progress I can see in my paintings keeps me motivated to continue. I hope that my artwork will further improve and develop such that one day, it might find its own way and justification.
